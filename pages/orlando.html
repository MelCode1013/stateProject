Orlando

A place that is well renowned for everyone's favorite mouse!

Population

In 2020, Orlando is recorded to have had 307,573 people living in the urban city, making it the fourth-most populous city in Florida. Many people come from all over to visit the most renown attractions, Disney World and Universal Studios. Not to mention the nightlife, bars, and clubs in Downtown Orlando. It's no wonder so many people come to visit as well as call Orlando their home.

Orlando's Economy & Tourism

Orlando is a huge industrial and tech center. The city is nationally recognized in its innovation in aerospace, aviation, and more. The low cost of doing business in Orlando is what attracts so many companies, which in turn, brings in more jobs. Tourism is rampant here due to many attractions such as:

  • Walt Disney World
  • Universal Studios
  • ICON Park
  • Dr. Phillips Center for the Performing Arts

Orlando's beginnings

In 1840, a small group of people grew up around Fort Gatlin, a fort built to protect settlers from Native American attacks during the Seminole war. The community was known as Jernigan, named after the Jernigan family. As the settlement expanded northward, the community changed its name to "Orlando".

Economy

Orlando's economy is booming, to say the least. Compared to other Florida cities such as Miami, Orlando boasts an employment rate that is 2.5% lower and a household income that is almost $10,000 higher according to data from BestPlaces (https://www.bestplaces.net/compare-cities/orlando_fl/miami_fl/economy). Located in Central Florida and near some big-name attractions, it is no wonder Orlando is a preferred place to make a living.
