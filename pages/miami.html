¡Buenvenidos a Miami!

Sandy beaches, beautiful people, night lifgie - Miami is the epitome of what people think of when they hear "Florida" (aside from Disney, of course!).

Population

As of the 2020 census, Miami is home to 442,241 people in South Florida. The majority of the city's population is Hispanic and Latino, making up 70.2% of the population. Having a density of 4,884.8/sq mi, this classifies miami as an urdan city. Many people flock to Miami for its Latin flair and vibrant nightlife, but in turn, causes overcrowding in this fast-growing city.

Miami's Economy

In 2016, Michelle Kaufman wrote an article in the Miami Herald titled "Is Miami such a bad place to live? It's 'the worst,' this study says". In the article, she states that the average home in Miami cost $245,000, while the national average was $181,200. And the average houseold income was only $31,917 - leaving families unable to afford housing.

Founding

Did you know Miami was actually founded by a woman named Jula Tuttle? She was a local citrus grower and the original owner of the land in which the city was built on. Her crops survived the Great Freeze of 1894-1895. This helped Miami growth tremedously and Miami was incorporated as a city on July 28, 1896.
